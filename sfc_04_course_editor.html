Identification Techniques for Sarawak Flora

Sarawak is home to one of the world's most diverse rainforest ecosystems, with over 8,000 species of flowering plants documented across its national parks and nature reserves.

Key Learning Objective: By the end of this lesson, you should be able to identify at least 10 protected plant species within your assigned park.

1. Visual Identification

  • Leaf shape and arrangement — alternate, opposite, whorled, or basal
  • Bark texture and colour — smooth, furrowed, scaly, or fibrous
  • Flower structure — number of petals, colour patterns, inflorescence type

2. Contextual Clues

The Rafflesia is exclusively found parasitising the roots of Tetrastigma vines in lowland forests.

Never touch or disturb any plant without authorisation. Collection is prohibited under the Wildlife Protection Ordinance 1998.
